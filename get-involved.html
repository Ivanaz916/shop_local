How to Get Involved

This site only works if neighbors like you actually use it — so the single most important thing you can do is start there.

Shoppers & Community Members

1

Shop local when the moment is right

Life is busy and complicated, and this site isn't here to guilt you out of the occasional Amazon order. Convenience matters, and sometimes a same-day delivery is exactly what you need. But for gifting, browsing, or those moments without a ticking clock — try here first. A local-first mindset doesn't have to mean local-first every time. Small shifts, made by enough people, move the needle.

2

Exchange with your neighbors

The circular economy section is built for exactly this — passing along what you no longer need, finding what someone nearby is offering. It's good for your wallet, good for the planet, and honestly, a pretty nice way to meet the people on your street.

3

Help keep things accurate

As the directory grows, it needs people who care enough to flag outdated info. If you notice a business listing that's stale — wrong hours, old contact details, a storefront that's closed — let us know. Keeping the directory trustworthy is a community effort.

4

Become a moderator

If you have a few hours a month and a stake in this community, consider helping moderate postings on the site. It's a small time commitment with a real impact on quality.

5

Become a storefronts outreach lead

As more businesses join, we'll need people to serve as local ambassadors — helping shops submit updated interior photos, keep their contact info current, and share what's actually on their shelves. If you're a people person who loves this town, this one's for you.

Shop Owners
